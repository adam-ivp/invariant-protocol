Honest answers
Does IVP prevent exploits?
No. Exploit transactions execute — the EVM is permissionless. IVP intercepts the drain phase. Most large exploits require multiple transactions. Guardian fires between the violation and the completed drain — reducing blast radius, not guaranteeing prevention.
Is IVP production-ready?
The invariant language, Guardian interface, and contract suite are built and security-patched. SP1 verifier integration is in progress. We are pre-testnet. We do not claim production readiness. Early integrators get 90 days free and direct access to the team.
Who controls the escrow?
The protocol's admin — not IVP. IVP provides the infrastructure. Protocols define their own release policy. IVP cannot unilaterally move funds. False positives are resolved via the dispute mechanism and returned automatically.
What if the invariant is wrong?
Disputes are bonded. Anyone can submit a counter-proof. If valid, the prover is slashed and Guardian fires onViolationCleared() — automatically unfreezing. Spec completeness is the protocol team's responsibility. IVP enforces what is committed.
Can provers collude with protocols?
It's economically mitigated, not cryptographically eliminated. The SpotChecker makes it operationally expensive: rotating private seed, commit-before-reveal, hidden checker identity. A prover cannot collude with someone they cannot identify. See specs/spot-checker-cryptography.md.
What are custom invariants?
Protocol-specific ISL specs written by the IVP team. Your protocol's exact security assumptions — storage layout, eMode mechanics, bridge architecture — encoded as formally verifiable constraints. This is where the bug bounty background is a direct product. We find the classes. We encode them.
